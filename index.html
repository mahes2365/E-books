Action and Adventure Books

Action and Adventure books constatly have you on the edge of your seat with excitement, as your fave main character repeatedly finds themselves in high stakes situations. The protagonist has an ultimate goal to achieve and is always put in risky, often dangerous situations.

Biographies and Autobiographies

Serving as an official account of the details and events of a person's life span, autobiographies are written by the subject themselves, while biographies are written by an author who is not the focus of the book.

Classical Books

You may think of these books as the throwback reading you were assigned in English class. The classics have been around for decades, and were often groundbreaking stories at their publish time, and serving as the foundation for many popular works we read today.

Comic Books

The stories in comic books and graphic novels are presented to the reader through engaging, sequential narrative art (illustration and typography) that's either presented in specific design or the traditional panel layout you find in comics.

Cooking Books

Traditionally penned by professional chefs or even your favorite celebs, cookbooks offer an appetizing collection of recipes, specific to a theme, cuisine, or experience chosen by the author.

Crime Books

Like its much-loved television conterparts, true crime books chronicle and examine actual crimes and events in exacting detail, with many focusing on infamous murders, kidnappings, and the exploits of serial killers.

Fantasy Books

While usually set in a fictional imagined world-in opposition. The fantasy books include prominent elements of magic, mythology, or the supernatural.

History Books

These books chronicle and layout a specific moment in time, with a goal to educate and inform the reader, looking at all parts of the world at any given moment.

Horror Books

Meant to cause discomfort and fear for both the character and readers, horror writers often make use supernatural and paranormal elements in morbid stories that are sometimes a little too realistic. The master of horror fiction? None other than Stephen King.

Literary Fiction Books

Though it can be seen as a broad genre that encompasses many others, literary fiction refers to the perceived artistic writing style of the author. Theri prose is meant to evoke deep thought through stories that offer personal or social commentary on a particular theme.

Detective and Mystery Books

The plot always revolves around a crime of sorts that must be solved-or foiled-by the protagonists.

Poetry

Poetry a form of written art from the author and choose a particular rhythm and style to evoke and portray various emotions and ideas. Sometimes the message is clear while with others, the meaning is hidden behind a play on words it all depends on the writer's style, intent, and chosen theme.

Romance

The genre that makes your heart all warm and fuzzy focuses on the love story of the main protagonists. This world of fiction is extremely wide-reaching in and of itself, as it has a variety of sug-genres including: contenmporary romance, historical, paranormal, and the steamier erotica.

Science Fiction (Sci-Fi)

Though They're often thought of in the same vein as fantasy, what distingusishes science fiction stories is that they lean heavily on themes of technology and future science.

Suspense and Thriller Books

While they often encompass the same elements as mystery books, the suspense and thriller genre sees the hero attempt to stop and defeat the villian to save theri own life rather than uncover a specific crime. Thrillers typically include cliffhangers and deception to encourage supense.
